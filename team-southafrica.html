Team South Africa

 The South Africa national cricket team, also known as the Proteas, represents South Africa in men's international cricket and is administered by Cricket South Africa.

 South Africa entered first-class and international cricket at the same time when they hosted an England cricket team in the 1888–89 season.

 The anti-apartheid movement led the ICC to impose a moratorium on tours in 1970, commensurate with actions taken by other global sporting bodies.

 The ban remained in place until 1991.The team has mostly been strong since reinstatement and has at times held number one positions in international rankings.

 The team is currently ranked 5th in both Test and ODIs and 6th in T20Is.
